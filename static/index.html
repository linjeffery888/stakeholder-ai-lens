About this prototype — methodology & honest limitations

What this is. A working prototype of an AI use-case discovery & prioritization pipeline, built for the AI Strategy Associate diagnostic. It ingests stakeholder interviews, extracts pain points, de-duplicates them into canonical use cases, enforces a savings-hypothesis gate, and ranks a prioritized portfolio with full provenance.

What is real vs. illustrative. Real: the pipeline itself — extraction, semantic de-duplication, the savings model, the gate, bounded RICE scoring, the labor/spend tracks, and traceable provenance. Point it at real interviews and it runs. Illustrative: the 100 interviews are synthetic, written to be realistic for a commercial cell-therapy company; they are not Iovance internal data. Organization spend figures are web-researched public estimates, used only to size the top-down spend track.

Known constraints & gaps (self-audit).

  • Savings are unvalidated estimates. Confidence is capped at 0.5 by design — nothing here has been proven with a controlled human-vs-AI comparison, which is the required next step before any number is committed.
  • Time and volume per task are estimates pulled from the synthetic transcripts, not measured. On real interviews the figures would move materially.
  • De-duplication is strong but imperfect. A live model clusters the pain points and a deterministic second pass merges residual duplicates; low-confidence merges are flagged for human review rather than trusted. Some over/under-merge can remain.
  • The spend track is top-down (addressable spend × waste × capture) — directional, pending a real spend/SaaS audit, and deliberately kept out of the headline number.
  • Net savings net out an assumed build + run cost. Precise per-use-case total cost of ownership is a post-selection exercise, not done here.
  • The gate catches clear non-addressable problems (headcount, capex, external timelines) but is not exhaustive.

Bottom line. This demonstrates the method and the working tool. Run on Iovance's actual stakeholder interviews, the structure holds and the numbers become the company's own.

How the Organization sizing works

Labor savings come from the interviews, not this panel. Each use case's labor opportunity is computed from the time × frequency stakeholders actually reported. Those volumes already reflect the organization's real scale, so editing this panel does not change them. To estimate for a larger company, you'd feed in its interviews and the labor scales through the data itself.

This panel sizes only the top-down spend-rationalization track: recoverable spend = addressable discretionary spend × waste rate × capture rate.

Does headcount affect labor? In the real world, yes — the labor opportunity grows with how many people perform a task. In this prototype that scaling already lives in the interview-reported volumes (time × frequency), so editing the headcount field here does not re-scale labor; a bigger company would surface bigger volumes through its own interviews. Headcount's one job in this panel is as a per-seat benchmark for software/SaaS spend.

Which spend scales with headcount?

  • Scales fairly linearly: per-seat software/SaaS and collaboration tools (~$8–12K per employee/yr).
  • Scales loosely: cloud infrastructure, discretionary vendors, professional services, contingent labor.
  • Does NOT scale this way (and is excluded): manufacturing materials, COGS, R&D / clinical-trial costs, and payroll — none are addressable by spend rationalization.

So to model another company: edit the addressable discretionary spend (or research it). The spend track resizes; the labor portfolio stays tied to the interviews.
