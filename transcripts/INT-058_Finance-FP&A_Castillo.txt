Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Controllership, FP&A, and cost accounting. COGS per batch is a huge focus given the gross-
  margin push.
- Leadership wants COGS down and margin up, so finance is under the microscope on cost per
  patient.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. COGS per batch is the number leadership cares about most and allocating cost to each
   patient batch is a painful manual exercise across systems.

2. Contract renewals sneak up on us because tracking them is a spreadsheet, and auto-renewals
   slip through and cost us.

3. Budget-versus-actual variance commentary is written from scratch each month even though
   half the explanations recur.

4. Month-end account reconciliation is where it hurts, an analyst ties ERP balances to
   subledgers and spreadsheets and chases every variance. A material account is 90 min and we
   do 500-600 a close.

5. Headcount approvals and the hiring-justification process are a governance flow that's slow
   by design, that's policy, not a tool gap.

6. Finding the right SOP or work instruction is a daily hunt, they're scattered across
   SharePoint and shared drives and the search never finds the current version.

7. Building the management reporting pack is its own slog, copying numbers out of five
   sources into a deck every month.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- If I could wave a wand and kill one thing, it's the manual report stitching.
