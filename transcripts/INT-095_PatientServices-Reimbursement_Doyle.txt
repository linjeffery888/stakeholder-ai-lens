Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Volume is climbing fast as more ATCs come online for Amtagvi.
- Team owns the patient journey from referral through scheduled infusion at the Authorized
  Treatment Center (ATC).

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. We burn hours just chasing prior-auth status, calling payers and sitting on hold to find
   out if something got approved. Pure waiting.

2. Reimbursement rates and the payer mix are what they are. The economics of a given case
   aren't something my team can fix, that's set above us.

3. We're capacity-limited on LN2 dry shippers and qualified packaging. That's procurement and
   hardware, you can't software your way to more shippers.

4. Honestly we just don't have enough coordinators for the case volume coming. More ATCs
   onboarding means more cases and we're already maxed. We need heads.

5. Patients and centers ask coverage questions constantly and we dig through plan policy PDFs
   to find the exact clause. The answer exists, finding it takes forever.

6. Scheduling a patient is calendar tetris: ATC availability, a manufacturing slot, the
   lymphodepletion window, and IL-2, all coordinated by hand across teams. One slip and the
   whole sequence reshuffles.

Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Flagged that whatever we build has to play nice with the validated systems or it won't get
