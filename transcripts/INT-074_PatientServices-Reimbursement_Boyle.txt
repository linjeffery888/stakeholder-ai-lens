Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- We sit between the prescribing center, the payer, and manufacturing. Nothing moves until
  benefits are confirmed.
- Team owns the patient journey from referral through scheduled infusion at the Authorized
  Treatment Center (ATC).

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Denial appeals are brutal. When a payer denies we draft the appeal more or less from
   scratch every time, people keep a few old letters and rewrite them, 45 min easy, and we do
   hundreds a year.

2. Biggest time sink is benefit verification. Before we can schedule anyone a coordinator
   pulls status out of the payer portal, our case system, and the copay tool and hand-
   assembles one picture of the case. Call it 30-40 min each and we're running thousands of
   cases a year, and it's error prone if one source is stale.

3. We burn hours just chasing prior-auth status, calling payers and sitting on hold to find
   out if something got approved. Pure waiting.

4. There's no single tracker of which payers cover Amtagvi and under what criteria. Policies
   change and we find out the hard way when something gets denied.

5. A lot of this job is emotional support for patients who are out of options. That's human
   work, you can't automate sitting with someone through a terminal diagnosis.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- If I could wave a wand and kill one thing, it's the manual report stitching.
