Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- We field the scientific questions the commercial team is not allowed to answer, plus case
  processing for safety.
- MSLs in the field, medical information desk, and the drug-safety/PV group handling adverse
  events.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Off-label questions from HCPs are a compliance minefield, how we respond is a judgment and
   legal line, not something to fully automate.

2. Every week I stitch together a status report from three or four different sources by hand,
   it's the same format every time and it still takes hours.

3. Adverse-event intake and case triage is high volume, reading in cases, coding them, and
   prioritizing, and the clock is regulatory so we can't fall behind.

4. The medical information desk drafts responses to HCP inquiries, and most questions repeat
   but each response is researched and written fresh against approved sources.

5. Literature surveillance for safety is a firehose, someone screens new publications weekly
   for anything relevant to our product.

6. MSLs build their own slide decks for every center visit, pulling from the master deck and
   tailoring it, hours each.

7. Actual safety signal detection takes clinical judgment, you can surface candidates but a
   human has to make the medical call.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- If I could wave a wand and kill one thing, it's the manual report stitching.
