Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- We run release and in-process testing: identity, potency, sterility, flow panels. Everything
  funnels through LIMS.
- Turnaround on testing is on the critical path to disposition, so any delay in QC delays a
  patient.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Instrument calibration and maintenance is physical and on a fixed schedule, that's
   metrology, not analytics.

2. Qualifying a new assay is bench science, you run the validation experiments, there's no
   automating the wet work.

3. Stability tracking is a manual calendar and spreadsheet exercise, what's due to pull, what
   was tested, what's trending.

4. We can't run more suites without more qualified operators, and qualifying an operator
   takes months. That's a people-and-time problem, not a software one.

5. Out-of-spec investigations are big writeups and we do a lot of them. Documenting the
   investigation is most of the effort.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- If I could wave a wand and kill one thing, it's the manual report stitching.
