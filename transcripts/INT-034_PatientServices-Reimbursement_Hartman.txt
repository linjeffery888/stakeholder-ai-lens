Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Team owns the patient journey from referral through scheduled infusion at the Authorized
  Treatment Center (ATC).
- Volume is climbing fast as more ATCs come online for Amtagvi.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Patients and centers ask coverage questions constantly and we dig through plan policy PDFs
   to find the exact clause. The answer exists, finding it takes forever.

2. Honestly we just don't have enough coordinators for the case volume coming. More ATCs
   onboarding means more cases and we're already maxed. We need heads.

3. Scheduling a patient is calendar tetris: ATC availability, a manufacturing slot, the
   lymphodepletion window, and IL-2, all coordinated by hand across teams. One slip and the
   whole sequence reshuffles.

4. Biggest time sink is benefit verification. Before we can schedule anyone a coordinator
   pulls status out of the payer portal, our case system, and the copay tool and hand-
   assembles one picture of the case. Call it 30-40 min each and we're running thousands of
   cases a year, and it's error prone if one source is stale.

5. So much knowledge is locked in people's heads and old email threads, when someone is out
   or leaves we lose the answer.

6. Reimbursement rates and the payer mix are what they are. The economics of a given case
   aren't something my team can fix, that's set above us.

7. We burn hours just chasing prior-auth status, calling payers and sitting on hold to find
   out if something got approved. Pure waiting.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Skeptical that a tool can handle the GxP documentation rigor, wants to see it before
  trusting it.
