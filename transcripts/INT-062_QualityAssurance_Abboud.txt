----------------------------------------------------------------
Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Quality owns batch disposition, deviations, change control, and audit readiness. For a
  personalized product the paperwork is the product.
- Every batch is patient-specific so the documentation burden per unit is brutal compared to
  small molecule.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Onboarding and keeping training material current is a constant background task, the
   documents go stale and nobody owns updating them.

2. We have hundreds of SOPs on periodic-review cycles. Someone has to read each one, decide
   if it still reflects practice, and document the review. It's relentless.

3. We genuinely need more trained QA reviewers, the volume is outpacing the team and review
   can't be skipped. That's headcount.

4. Change control documentation is heavy, every change needs an impact assessment written,
   routed, and tracked, and the writing is repetitive.

5. The actual timing of FDA and EMA decisions is completely outside our control, we can
   prepare but we can't move their calendar.

6. Annual Product Quality Review compilation is a multi-week effort pulling data from
   everywhere into one report.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Noted the volume is only going up as manufacturing internalizes and more markets come
  online.
