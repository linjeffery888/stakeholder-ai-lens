Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Team owns the patient journey from referral through scheduled infusion at the Authorized
  Treatment Center (ATC).
- We sit between the prescribing center, the payer, and manufacturing. Nothing moves until
  benefits are confirmed.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Scheduling a patient is calendar tetris: ATC availability, a manufacturing slot, the
   lymphodepletion window, and IL-2, all coordinated by hand across teams. One slip and the
   whole sequence reshuffles.

2. A lot of this job is emotional support for patients who are out of options. That's human
   work, you can't automate sitting with someone through a terminal diagnosis.

3. So much knowledge is locked in people's heads and old email threads, when someone is out
   or leaves we lose the answer.

4. Honestly we just don't have enough coordinators for the case volume coming. More ATCs
   onboarding means more cases and we're already maxed. We need heads.

5. Denial appeals are brutal. When a payer denies we draft the appeal more or less from
   scratch every time, people keep a few old letters and rewrite them, 45 min easy, and we do
   hundreds a year.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Noted the volume is only going up as manufacturing internalizes and more markets come
  online.
