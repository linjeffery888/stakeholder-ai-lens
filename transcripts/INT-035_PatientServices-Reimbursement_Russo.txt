Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Team owns the patient journey from referral through scheduled infusion at the Authorized
  Treatment Center (ATC).
- We sit between the prescribing center, the payer, and manufacturing. Nothing moves until
  benefits are confirmed.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Scheduling a patient is calendar tetris: ATC availability, a manufacturing slot, the
   lymphodepletion window, and IL-2, all coordinated by hand across teams. One slip and the
   whole sequence reshuffles.

2. Biggest time sink is benefit verification. Before we can schedule anyone a coordinator
   pulls status out of the payer portal, our case system, and the copay tool and hand-
   assembles one picture of the case. Call it 30-40 min each and we're running thousands of
   cases a year, and it's error prone if one source is stale.

3. A lot of this job is emotional support for patients who are out of options. That's human
   work, you can't automate sitting with someone through a terminal diagnosis.

4. Patients and centers ask coverage questions constantly and we dig through plan policy PDFs
   to find the exact clause. The answer exists, finding it takes forever.

5. Finding the right SOP or work instruction is a daily hunt, they're scattered across
   SharePoint and shared drives and the search never finds the current version.

6. Reimbursement rates and the payer mix are what they are. The economics of a given case
   aren't something my team can fix, that's set above us.

Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Noted the volume is only going up as manufacturing internalizes and more markets come
