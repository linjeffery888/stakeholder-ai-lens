Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Compliance training content has to be built and refreshed constantly, and it's mostly
   assembling and rewording existing material.

2. A real privacy or HIPAA incident is a judgment-and-liability call, you escalate to people,
   you don't let a model decide.

3. Onboarding and keeping training material current is a constant background task, the
   documents go stale and nobody owns updating them.

4. Drafting and updating policies and SOPs is slow, lots of cross-referencing and
   consistency-checking.

5. Actual negotiation and litigation strategy is judgment and relationships, that's the core
   lawyering and it stays human.

6. Finding a prior contract or a specific clause in the repository is a nightmare, the search
   is bad so we re-draft things we already have.

7. NDA and CDA turnaround is a bottleneck for the whole company, partners wait on us and
   they're 80% boilerplate.
