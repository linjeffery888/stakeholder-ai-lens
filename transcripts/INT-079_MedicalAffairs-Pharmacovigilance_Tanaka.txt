Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- MSLs in the field, medical information desk, and the drug-safety/PV group handling adverse
  events.
- We field the scientific questions the commercial team is not allowed to answer, plus case
  processing for safety.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Adverse-event intake and case triage is high volume, reading in cases, coding them, and
   prioritizing, and the clock is regulatory so we can't fall behind.

2. The medical information desk drafts responses to HCP inquiries, and most questions repeat
   but each response is researched and written fresh against approved sources.

3. Off-label questions from HCPs are a compliance minefield, how we respond is a judgment and
   legal line, not something to fully automate.

4. Drafting adverse-event narratives for the safety database is repetitive structured
   writing, and we do a lot of them.

5. Actual safety signal detection takes clinical judgment, you can surface candidates but a
   human has to make the medical call.

6. Literature surveillance for safety is a firehose, someone screens new publications weekly
   for anything relevant to our product.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Open to piloting something if it's reliable and stays inside our validated systems.
