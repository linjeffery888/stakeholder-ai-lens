Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- We run the trials: the sarcoma registrational study, endometrial, and the next-gen gene-
  edited TIL programs.
- Oversight of CROs and sites, plus all the trial documentation that has to be inspection-
  ready.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Health-authority queries come in with tight clocks and we draft responses pulling from
   prior submissions and source docs every time.

2. Reviewing monitoring visit reports from CRAs is a steady load, reading long reports and
   pulling out the issues that need action.

3. Site startup drags on IRB and contract timelines that are external, no internal tool
   speeds up another institution's committee.

4. Enrollment forecasting is guesswork in a spreadsheet, and when a model would help, the
   underlying problem is still that these are rare tumors.

5. Slow enrollment is fundamentally a biology and epidemiology problem, the patients are rare
   and you can't manufacture eligible patients.

6. Reconciling CRO-delivered data against our own trackers is endless, the numbers never
   quite match and we chase the differences.

7. Trial master file completeness is a constant audit risk, someone is forever checking which
   documents are missing or expired across thousands of files.

Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Flagged that whatever we build has to play nice with the validated systems or it won't get
  approved.
