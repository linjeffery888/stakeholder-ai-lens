Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Every batch is patient-specific so the documentation burden per unit is brutal compared to
  small molecule.
- Quality owns batch disposition, deviations, change control, and audit readiness. For a
  personalized product the paperwork is the product.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Annual Product Quality Review compilation is a multi-week effort pulling data from
   everywhere into one report.

2. So much knowledge is locked in people's heads and old email threads, when someone is out
   or leaves we lose the answer.

3. We genuinely need more trained QA reviewers, the volume is outpacing the team and review
   can't be skipped. That's headcount.

4. Environmental monitoring generates a mountain of data entry, readings keyed in by hand
   into the system every shift.

5. Deviations and CAPAs are the bottleneck. Authoring and reviewing them is a backlog that
   never clears, each one is a structured writeup and we have hundreds open at any time.

6. Inspection readiness is also a culture and behavior thing on the floor, that's leadership
   and training, not a tool.

7. Change control documentation is heavy, every change needs an impact assessment written,
   routed, and tracked, and the writing is repetitive.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Wants any solution to keep a human in the loop for anything patient-impacting.
