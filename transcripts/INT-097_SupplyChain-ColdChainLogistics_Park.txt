Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Chain of identity and chain of custody is everything. A single mislabeled handoff is a
  patient-impacting event.
- We orchestrate the cold chain both directions: fresh tumor inbound, cryopreserved product
  outbound to the ATC.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Inbound tumor tissue has to land in a tight window and we hand-coordinate every pickup
   against the manufacturing slot. Constant re-planning.

2. Batch record review is the slog. For every patient batch someone reviews the electronic
   batch record line by line for completeness before it goes to QA, and any blank or
   transcription miss bounces it back. It's hours per batch and every batch is a patient.

3. We're capacity-limited on LN2 dry shippers and qualified packaging. That's procurement and
   hardware, you can't software your way to more shippers.

4. Finding the right SOP or work instruction is a daily hunt, they're scattered across
   SharePoint and shared drives and the search never finds the current version.

5. Flights, weather, and customs are out of our hands. No model makes a grounded plane fly,
   that's physical-world risk we just manage.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Noted the volume is only going up as manufacturing internalizes and more markets come
  online.
