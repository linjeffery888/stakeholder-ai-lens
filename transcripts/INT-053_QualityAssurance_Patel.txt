Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Quality owns batch disposition, deviations, change control, and audit readiness. For a
  personalized product the paperwork is the product.
- Every batch is patient-specific so the documentation burden per unit is brutal compared to
  small molecule.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Supplier quality complaints need trending and we do it manually across a year of records.

2. We genuinely need more trained QA reviewers, the volume is outpacing the team and review
   can't be skipped. That's headcount.

3. Inspection readiness is also a culture and behavior thing on the floor, that's leadership
   and training, not a tool.

4. Deviations and CAPAs are the bottleneck. Authoring and reviewing them is a backlog that
   never clears, each one is a structured writeup and we have hundreds open at any time.

5. Audit and inspection prep is a fire drill every time, we scramble to gather documents and
   build the narrative from scattered sources.

6. We have hundreds of SOPs on periodic-review cycles. Someone has to read each one, decide
   if it still reflects practice, and document the review. It's relentless.

- Open to piloting something if it's reliable and stays inside our validated systems.
