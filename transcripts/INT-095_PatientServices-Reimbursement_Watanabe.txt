Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- We sit between the prescribing center, the payer, and manufacturing. Nothing moves until
  benefits are confirmed.
- Volume is climbing fast as more ATCs come online for Amtagvi.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Honestly we just don't have enough coordinators for the case volume coming. More ATCs
   onboarding means more cases and we're already maxed. We need heads.

2. We burn hours just chasing prior-auth status, calling payers and sitting on hold to find
   out if something got approved. Pure waiting.

3. Denial appeals are brutal. When a payer denies we draft the appeal more or less from
   scratch every time, people keep a few old letters and rewrite them, 45 min easy, and we do
   hundreds a year.

4. Biggest time sink is benefit verification. Before we can schedule anyone a coordinator
   pulls status out of the payer portal, our case system, and the copay tool and hand-
   assembles one picture of the case. Call it 30-40 min each and we're running thousands of
   cases a year, and it's error prone if one source is stale.

5. Scheduling a patient is calendar tetris: ATC availability, a manufacturing slot, the
   lymphodepletion window, and IL-2, all coordinated by hand across teams. One slip and the
   whole sequence reshuffles.

6. A lot of this job is emotional support for patients who are out of options. That's human
   work, you can't automate sitting with someone through a terminal diagnosis.

7. Every week I stitch together a status report from three or four different sources by hand,
   it's the same format every time and it still takes hours.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- If I could wave a wand and kill one thing, it's the manual report stitching.
