Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Volume is climbing fast as more ATCs come online for Amtagvi.
- We sit between the prescribing center, the payer, and manufacturing. Nothing moves until
  benefits are confirmed.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. We burn hours just chasing prior-auth status, calling payers and sitting on hold to find
   out if something got approved. Pure waiting.

2. So much knowledge is locked in people's heads and old email threads, when someone is out
   or leaves we lose the answer.

3. There's no single tracker of which payers cover Amtagvi and under what criteria. Policies
   change and we find out the hard way when something gets denied.

4. Reimbursement rates and the payer mix are what they are. The economics of a given case
   aren't something my team can fix, that's set above us.

5. Denial appeals are brutal. When a payer denies we draft the appeal more or less from
   scratch every time, people keep a few old letters and rewrite them, 45 min easy, and we do
   hundreds a year.

6. Honestly we just don't have enough coordinators for the case volume coming. More ATCs
   onboarding means more cases and we're already maxed. We need heads.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- If I could wave a wand and kill one thing, it's the manual report stitching.
