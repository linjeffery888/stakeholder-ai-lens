Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Team owns the patient journey from referral through scheduled infusion at the Authorized
  Treatment Center (ATC).
- We sit between the prescribing center, the payer, and manufacturing. Nothing moves until
  benefits are confirmed.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Denial appeals are brutal. When a payer denies we draft the appeal more or less from
   scratch every time, people keep a few old letters and rewrite them, 45 min easy, and we do
   hundreds a year.

2. Onboarding and keeping training material current is a constant background task, the
   documents go stale and nobody owns updating them.

3. Patients and centers ask coverage questions constantly and we dig through plan policy PDFs
   to find the exact clause. The answer exists, finding it takes forever.

4. Competitive intelligence is a person reading press releases and conference coverage and
   writing it up, it's never current.

5. There's no single tracker of which payers cover Amtagvi and under what criteria. Policies
   change and we find out the hard way when something gets denied.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Says the team would welcome the help but worries about anything touching release decisions.
