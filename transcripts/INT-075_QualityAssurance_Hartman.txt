----------------------------------------------------------------
Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Quality owns batch disposition, deviations, change control, and audit readiness. For a
  personalized product the paperwork is the product.
- Every batch is patient-specific so the documentation burden per unit is brutal compared to
  small molecule.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. We have hundreds of SOPs on periodic-review cycles. Someone has to read each one, decide
   if it still reflects practice, and document the review. It's relentless.

2. Supplier quality complaints need trending and we do it manually across a year of records.

3. We genuinely need more trained QA reviewers, the volume is outpacing the team and review
   can't be skipped. That's headcount.

4. Batch disposition review means a QA person re-reads the entire batch record and supporting
   docs before release. For a patient-specific product that's enormous and it's on the
   patient's critical path.

5. Deviations and CAPAs are the bottleneck. Authoring and reviewing them is a backlog that
   never clears, each one is a structured writeup and we have hundreds open at any time.

6. Audit and inspection prep is a fire drill every time, we scramble to gather documents and
   build the narrative from scattered sources.

7. So much knowledge is locked in people's heads and old email threads, when someone is out
   or leaves we lose the answer.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Flagged that whatever we build has to play nice with the validated systems or it won't get
  approved.
