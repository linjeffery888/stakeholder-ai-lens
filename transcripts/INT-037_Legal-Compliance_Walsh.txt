Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Small team, very high contract throughput, plus the healthcare-compliance overhead of a
  commercial pharma.
- Contracts, compliance, and policy. Heavy CDA/MSA volume with CROs, ATCs, suppliers, and
  academic collaborators.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Actual negotiation and litigation strategy is judgment and relationships, that's the core
   lawyering and it stays human.

2. Monitoring healthcare-compliance obligations like Sunshine Act reporting is partly
   tracking we could speed up and partly judgment.

3. Finding a prior contract or a specific clause in the repository is a nightmare, the search
   is bad so we re-draft things we already have.

4. NDA and CDA turnaround is a bottleneck for the whole company, partners wait on us and
   they're 80% boilerplate.

5. Contract review eats the team, redlining standard CDAs, MSAs, and work orders against our
   playbook, most of it the same edits over and over.

6. Drafting and updating policies and SOPs is slow, lots of cross-referencing and
   consistency-checking.

7. Compliance training content has to be built and refreshed constantly, and it's mostly
   assembling and rewording existing material.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Noted the volume is only going up as manufacturing internalizes and more markets come
  online.
