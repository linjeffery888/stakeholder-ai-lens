Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Every batch is patient-specific so the documentation burden per unit is brutal compared to
  small molecule.
- Quality owns batch disposition, deviations, change control, and audit readiness. For a
  personalized product the paperwork is the product.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Deviations and CAPAs are the bottleneck. Authoring and reviewing them is a backlog that
   never clears, each one is a structured writeup and we have hundreds open at any time.

2. Change control documentation is heavy, every change needs an impact assessment written,
   routed, and tracked, and the writing is repetitive.

3. Finding the right SOP or work instruction is a daily hunt, they're scattered across
   SharePoint and shared drives and the search never finds the current version.

4. Audit and inspection prep is a fire drill every time, we scramble to gather documents and
   build the narrative from scattered sources.

5. We genuinely need more trained QA reviewers, the volume is outpacing the team and review
   can't be skipped. That's headcount.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- If I could wave a wand and kill one thing, it's the manual report stitching.
