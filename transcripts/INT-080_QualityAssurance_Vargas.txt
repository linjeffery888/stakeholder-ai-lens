Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Quality owns batch disposition, deviations, change control, and audit readiness. For a
  personalized product the paperwork is the product.
- Every batch is patient-specific so the documentation burden per unit is brutal compared to
  small molecule.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Supplier quality complaints need trending and we do it manually across a year of records.

2. Every week I stitch together a status report from three or four different sources by hand,
   it's the same format every time and it still takes hours.

3. Change control documentation is heavy, every change needs an impact assessment written,
   routed, and tracked, and the writing is repetitive.

4. Inspection readiness is also a culture and behavior thing on the floor, that's leadership
   and training, not a tool.

5. We have hundreds of SOPs on periodic-review cycles. Someone has to read each one, decide
   if it still reflects practice, and document the review. It's relentless.

6. We genuinely need more trained QA reviewers, the volume is outpacing the team and review
   can't be skipped. That's headcount.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Wants any solution to keep a human in the loop for anything patient-impacting.
