Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Quality owns batch disposition, deviations, change control, and audit readiness. For a
  personalized product the paperwork is the product.
- Every batch is patient-specific so the documentation burden per unit is brutal compared to
  small molecule.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Batch disposition review means a QA person re-reads the entire batch record and supporting
   docs before release. For a patient-specific product that's enormous and it's on the
   patient's critical path.

2. Annual Product Quality Review compilation is a multi-week effort pulling data from
   everywhere into one report.

3. Audit and inspection prep is a fire drill every time, we scramble to gather documents and
   build the narrative from scattered sources.

4. We have hundreds of SOPs on periodic-review cycles. Someone has to read each one, decide
   if it still reflects practice, and document the review. It's relentless.

5. Change control documentation is heavy, every change needs an impact assessment written,
   routed, and tracked, and the writing is repetitive.

6. Deviations and CAPAs are the bottleneck. Authoring and reviewing them is a backlog that
   never clears, each one is a structured writeup and we have hundreds open at any time.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Open to piloting something if it's reliable and stays inside our validated systems.
