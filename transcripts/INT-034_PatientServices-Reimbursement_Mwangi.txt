Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- We sit between the prescribing center, the payer, and manufacturing. Nothing moves until
  benefits are confirmed.
- Volume is climbing fast as more ATCs come online for Amtagvi.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Biggest time sink is benefit verification. Before we can schedule anyone a coordinator
   pulls status out of the payer portal, our case system, and the copay tool and hand-
   assembles one picture of the case. Call it 30-40 min each and we're running thousands of
   cases a year, and it's error prone if one source is stale.

2. Denial appeals are brutal. When a payer denies we draft the appeal more or less from
   scratch every time, people keep a few old letters and rewrite them, 45 min easy, and we do
   hundreds a year.

3. Patients and centers ask coverage questions constantly and we dig through plan policy PDFs
   to find the exact clause. The answer exists, finding it takes forever.

4. We burn hours just chasing prior-auth status, calling payers and sitting on hold to find
   out if something got approved. Pure waiting.

5. Honestly we just don't have enough coordinators for the case volume coming. More ATCs
   onboarding means more cases and we're already maxed. We need heads.

6. So much knowledge is locked in people's heads and old email threads, when someone is out
   or leaves we lose the answer.

7. Scheduling a patient is calendar tetris: ATC availability, a manufacturing slot, the
   lymphodepletion window, and IL-2, all coordinated by hand across teams. One slip and the
   whole sequence reshuffles.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Wants any solution to keep a human in the loop for anything patient-impacting.
