Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Internalizing all manufacturing in-house at the iCTC, so volume is ramping hard.
- Second-gen process, turnaround down around 22-34 days. The slot is the constraint, not the
  chemistry on a good day.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Chain-of-identity checks are manual label reconciliation at every step. Necessary, but a
   person is eyeballing IDs and initialing, over and over.

2. Some of our incubators and equipment are aging and we get unplanned downtime. That needs
   capital and new hardware, not an app.

3. Scheduling manufacturing slots against incoming tumor tissue is a constant puzzle. Tissue
   arrives in tight windows, suites and staff are fixed, and we re-plan by hand whenever
   anything shifts.

4. Shift handoff is all manual notes. Night shift writes up where each batch stands and day
   shift re-reads it, and stuff gets lost in the translation.

5. Onboarding and keeping training material current is a constant background task, the
   documents go stale and nobody owns updating them.

6. Yield still varies batch to batch because it's living cells from a sick patient. No model
   fixes the underlying biology, the starting material is what it is.

7. We can't run more suites without more qualified operators, and qualifying an operator
   takes months. That's a people-and-time problem, not a software one.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Wants any solution to keep a human in the loop for anything patient-impacting.
