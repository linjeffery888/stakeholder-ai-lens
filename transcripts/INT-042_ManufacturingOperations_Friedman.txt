Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Autologous TIL, so every batch is one patient. Tumor tissue comes in, we expand to billions
  of cells, cryopreserve, ship back.
- Second-gen process, turnaround down around 22-34 days. The slot is the constraint, not the
  chemistry on a good day.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Some of our incubators and equipment are aging and we get unplanned downtime. That needs
   capital and new hardware, not an app.

2. Deviation writeups eat the floor. When something goes off-script the associate documents
   it in detail, and they're doing it mid-shift trying to remember exactly what happened.
   Slow and inconsistent.

3. Deviations and CAPAs are the bottleneck. Authoring and reviewing them is a backlog that
   never clears, each one is a structured writeup and we have hundreds open at any time.

4. Onboarding and keeping training material current is a constant background task, the
   documents go stale and nobody owns updating them.

5. Scheduling manufacturing slots against incoming tumor tissue is a constant puzzle. Tissue
   arrives in tight windows, suites and staff are fixed, and we re-plan by hand whenever
   anything shifts.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Asked what they'd hand off first: the repetitive writing, no question.
