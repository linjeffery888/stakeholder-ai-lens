Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- We orchestrate the cold chain both directions: fresh tumor inbound, cryopreserved product
  outbound to the ATC.
- Chain of identity and chain of custody is everything. A single mislabeled handoff is a
  patient-impacting event.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Courier and vendor performance reporting is manual every quarter, pulling shipment data
   and building the scorecards by hand.

2. Inbound tumor tissue has to land in a tight window and we hand-coordinate every pickup
   against the manufacturing slot. Constant re-planning.

3. We're capacity-limited on LN2 dry shippers and qualified packaging. That's procurement and
   hardware, you can't software your way to more shippers.

4. Demand forecasting for consumables is basically a person's gut plus a spreadsheet, and
   when we're wrong we either stock out or sit on expensive expiring material.

5. Flights, weather, and customs are out of our hands. No model makes a grounded plane fly,
   that's physical-world risk we just manage.

6. Materials and consumables inventory never reconciles cleanly between the warehouse system
   and the ERP, so someone goes line by line monthly.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- If I could wave a wand and kill one thing, it's the manual report stitching.
