Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Lots of parallel markets now: US approved, Australia conditional, EMA resubmission in
  progress, Switzerland pending.
- We own submissions and health-authority interactions across the markets where Amtagvi is
  approved or pending.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. The actual timing of FDA and EMA decisions is completely outside our control, we can
   prepare but we can't move their calendar.

2. Some ex-US submissions need certified translations and local agent work that's inherently
   external and manual.

3. Assembling submissions is heavy lifting, compiling modules, cross-referencing, formatting
   to each authority's spec. Hours of assembly per submission.

4. Health-authority queries come in with tight clocks and we draft responses pulling from
   prior submissions and source docs every time.

5. Labeling changes have to be managed across regions and reconciled against local
   requirements, very manual cross-checking.

6. Keeping up with new guidances and summarizing what they mean for us is a reading-and-
   writing job nobody has time for.

7. Tracking regulatory commitments across all our markets is a spreadsheet that's always
   slightly out of date, and missing one is a serious problem.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- If I could wave a wand and kill one thing, it's the manual report stitching.
