Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Every batch is patient-specific so the documentation burden per unit is brutal compared to
  small molecule.
- Quality owns batch disposition, deviations, change control, and audit readiness. For a
  personalized product the paperwork is the product.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. So much knowledge is locked in people's heads and old email threads, when someone is out
   or leaves we lose the answer.

2. Inspection readiness is also a culture and behavior thing on the floor, that's leadership
   and training, not a tool.

3. We have hundreds of SOPs on periodic-review cycles. Someone has to read each one, decide
   if it still reflects practice, and document the review. It's relentless.

4. We genuinely need more trained QA reviewers, the volume is outpacing the team and review
   can't be skipped. That's headcount.

5. Annual Product Quality Review compilation is a multi-week effort pulling data from
   everywhere into one report.

6. Change control documentation is heavy, every change needs an impact assessment written,
   routed, and tracked, and the writing is repetitive.

Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Says the team would welcome the help but worries about anything touching release decisions.
