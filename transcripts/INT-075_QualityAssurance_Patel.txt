Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Quality owns batch disposition, deviations, change control, and audit readiness. For a
  personalized product the paperwork is the product.
- Every batch is patient-specific so the documentation burden per unit is brutal compared to
  small molecule.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Annual Product Quality Review compilation is a multi-week effort pulling data from
   everywhere into one report.

2. We have hundreds of SOPs on periodic-review cycles. Someone has to read each one, decide
   if it still reflects practice, and document the review. It's relentless.

3. Deviations and CAPAs are the bottleneck. Authoring and reviewing them is a backlog that
   never clears, each one is a structured writeup and we have hundreds open at any time.

4. Audit and inspection prep is a fire drill every time, we scramble to gather documents and
   build the narrative from scattered sources.

5. Batch disposition review means a QA person re-reads the entire batch record and supporting
   docs before release. For a patient-specific product that's enormous and it's on the
   patient's critical path.

6. Inspection readiness is also a culture and behavior thing on the floor, that's leadership
   and training, not a tool.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Wants any solution to keep a human in the loop for anything patient-impacting.
