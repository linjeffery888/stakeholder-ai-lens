Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- We run release and in-process testing: identity, potency, sterility, flow panels. Everything
  funnels through LIMS.
- Turnaround on testing is on the critical path to disposition, so any delay in QC delays a
  patient.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Onboarding and keeping training material current is a constant background task, the
   documents go stale and nobody owns updating them.

2. Reviewing certificates of analysis and transcribing results into LIMS is constant, and any
   transcription error is a deviation, so it gets double-checked, doubling the work.

3. Qualifying a new assay is bench science, you run the validation experiments, there's no
   automating the wet work.

4. Out-of-spec investigations are big writeups and we do a lot of them. Documenting the
   investigation is most of the effort.

5. Sample login gets backed up at peak, everything keyed in manually before testing can even
   start.

6. Stability tracking is a manual calendar and spreadsheet exercise, what's due to pull, what
   was tested, what's trending.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Noted the volume is only going up as manufacturing internalizes and more markets come
  online.
