Background / current role
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Volume is climbing fast as more ATCs come online for Amtagvi.
- We sit between the prescribing center, the payer, and manufacturing. Nothing moves until
  benefits are confirmed.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Pain points / friction raised (in their words)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. A lot of this job is emotional support for patients who are out of options. That's human
   work, you can't automate sitting with someone through a terminal diagnosis.

2. We burn hours just chasing prior-auth status, calling payers and sitting on hold to find
   out if something got approved. Pure waiting.

3. Reimbursement rates and the payer mix are what they are. The economics of a given case
   aren't something my team can fix, that's set above us.

4. Honestly we just don't have enough coordinators for the case volume coming. More ATCs
   onboarding means more cases and we're already maxed. We need heads.

5. Patients and centers ask coverage questions constantly and we dig through plan policy PDFs
   to find the exact clause. The answer exists, finding it takes forever.

6. Denial appeals are brutal. When a payer denies we draft the appeal more or less from
   scratch every time, people keep a few old letters and rewrite them, 45 min easy, and we do
   hundreds a year.

7. Scheduling a patient is calendar tetris: ATC availability, a manufacturing slot, the
   lymphodepletion window, and IL-2, all coordinated by hand across teams. One slip and the
   whole sequence reshuffles.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Closing note
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Flagged that whatever we build has to play nice with the validated systems or it won't get
  approved.
